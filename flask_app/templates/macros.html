Glossary and Explanation

So what exactly is shown?

Everything is derived from the score difference of each game. The only information going into the model is the final score difference, the ten players on the court, and a few variables for fatigue/rest and first possession. A time decay half-life of 270 days means games gradually lose weight over time.

Interpretation: “Player X has a rating of 1.5, so that player makes the team 1.5 points better than an average replacement.”

How the ratings are formed

Consider a game with four below-average teammates and five slightly above-average opponents. The expected point differential is determined by those nine players, and performance relative to that expectation drives rating changes.

For this reason it is neither inherently good nor bad to have better or worse teammates or opponents. The only barometer is performance versus expectation (the other nine players).

Stats

Proj Score Diff – Sum of team ratings minus opponent ratings.

Teammate Quality – Sum of teammate ratings (excluding the player).

Team Quality – Sum of team ratings.

Opp Quality – Sum of opponent ratings.

Pct Positive Teammates – Share of games where teammates are net positive.

Pct Positive Opps – Share of games where opponents are net positive.

Pct Games as Favorite – Share of games where the team is favored.

Pct Games w/ Better Teammates – Share of games with better teammates than opponents.

Other 9 Players Quality Diff – Average difference between teammate and opponent ratings.

First Game Day Rate – Percent of days the player appears in the first game.

Last Game Day Rate – Percent of days the player appears in the last game.

Games

Team X Quality – Sum of team player ratings.

Spread – Team A Quality minus Team B Quality.

Diff From Spread – Actual score difference minus spread.

Abs – Absolute value of the difference.

Team A Win Prob – Win probability from a logistic regression of team qualities.

Moneyline – Win probability converted to moneyline.

Player Days

First Game Day – Game number where the player first appeared that day.

Last Game Day – Game number where the player last appeared that day.

Longest Run on Court/Bench – Longest consecutive stretch playing or sitting.

Player Pairings

Teammate / Other 9 Players Quality – Ratings with the two listed players removed.

Days

Mean Rating Players – Mean rating of all players present, weighted equally.

Mean Rating Player Games – Mean rating of all player-games.

Longest Run on Court/Bench – Maximum consecutive games for any player that day.

Avg Longest Run on Court/Bench – Average of each player’s longest run.

Unique Winners Rate – Share of attendees who won at least one game.

Avg Parity of Teammates – Standard deviation of teammate ratings (lower is more even).

Avg Parity of Teams – Standard deviation of team ratings.

Avg Parity of Score Diff/Spread/Win Probs – Standard deviation of those metrics.

Methodology

Overview

This is a RAPM-style model, inspired by work such as Jacob Cutter’s RAPM writeup.

Basic plus-minus measures point differential while a player is on the floor. Adjusted plus-minus uses regression to control for the other nine players, attributing impact more fairly. Regularized adjusted plus-minus (RAPM) uses ridge regression to shrink coefficients, stabilizing estimates for small samples.

Conceptual chain

Game results → score differential → minutes and on/off stints → regression with one regressor per player → ridge regularization → per-player ratings representing impact in points per game.
